Domain-Driven Development in real world

2020-04-29

Reading several books and articles about Domain-Driven Development (DDD), I’ve felt that DDD is quite hard to apply to the real world. Because it requires strong consensuses between people working together. So I’ve wanted to turn DDD into something that we can physically see. Here’s how I did.

DDD’s core is composed of:

  • Contexts
  • Contextual boundaries

Because they define domains. A domain is a set of perceptions of a thing and use cases, which are derived by a context and its boundaries to the related contexts.

To enable "touchable DDD”, at the organization-level, you can have small teams that handle different contexts (domains). Maybe you want to make a team to handle multiple contexts as team’s capacity is larger than a context’s cost. Similarly, at code-level, we can have a package to separate a context.

You might have noticed it already, but this is not different from what we’re doing. What I want to say here is that you just didn’t know the fitting size of a context or you didn’t even define a context clearly. Such as an unintended monolith that you often suffer from.
